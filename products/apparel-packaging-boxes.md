## 2. A Deep Dive into Material Selection: The Foundation of Luxury

The first step in creating a world-class **apparel gift box** is selecting the right materials. As a factory-direct provider, we offer an extensive range of boards and wraps that form the backbone of your brand's physical presence.

### The Core: High-Density Greyboard
The strength of a **sturdy rigid construction** comes from the core material: greyboard (also known as chipboard or binder board).
- **1200gsm (1.5mm):** Ideal for smaller apparel boxes, like ties, scarves, or light t-shirts. It provides a solid feel without excessive weight.
- **1500gsm (2.0mm):** The industry standard for luxury clothing boxes. It offers significant crush resistance and a premium weight.
- **2000gsm (2.5mm):** Reserved for heavy textiles, outerwear, or high-end footwear boxes. This thickness ensures the box remains rigid even when stacked in bulk during warehouse storage.

### The Wrap: Elevating the Aesthetic
The outer wrap is what your customer sees and feels. We offer:
1. **Art Paper (C2S):** The most versatile option. It has a smooth coating that allows for vibrant, high-definition printing. We typically apply a matte or gloss lamination to protect the print from scuffs.
2. **Natural Kraft Paper:** For brands with a sustainable or "organic" identity. Kraft paper offers a textured, earthy feel and is highly durable.
3. **Specialty Textured Papers:** These papers are dyed-through (meaning the color is consistent throughout the material) and often feature embossed patterns like linen, leather, or wood grain. They provide a tactile experience that standard printing cannot replicate.
4. **Soft-Touch Film:** A thin lamination that gives the box a velvety, suede-like feel. It is a popular choice for minimalist, high-end fashion brands.

## 4. Advanced Finishing Techniques: The Final Touch

Beyond basic printing, our factory employs advanced techniques to ensure your **custom apparel packaging** is truly unique.

### Foil Stamping vs. Digital Foil
- **Traditional Foil Stamping:** Uses a heated metal die to press foil into the paper. This creates a slight indentation and a crisp, brilliant metallic finish. It is ideal for high-volume runs where precision is paramount.
- **Digital Foil:** Does not require a die, making it cost-effective for smaller runs or variable data. While it lacks the physical indentation of traditional stamping, it offers a perfectly flat, shiny finish.

### Embossing and Debossing
These techniques create a three-dimensional effect. **Embossing** raises the logo above the surface, while **debossing** presses it in. When combined with foil stamping, the result is a stunning, multi-layered branding element.

### Spot UV and Glitter Coatings
For a more modern aesthetic, Spot UV can be used to add a high-gloss contrast to matte-finished boxes. This is particularly effective for subtle, repeating patterns of your logo across the box lid.

## 7. Structural Engineering for the Shipping Cycle

A beautiful box is useless if it arrives damaged. Our **sturdy rigid construction** is engineered to survive the global supply chain.

### The Science of Crush Resistance
When shipping luxury items internationally, packaging must withstand being stacked, compressed, and dropped. We utilize vertical compression tests to ensure that our 1500gsm and 2000gsm boards provide the necessary protection. By using high-density recycled greyboard, we offer a product that is not only strong but also structurally consistent.

### Magnetic Closure Engineering
The "snap" of a magnetic closure is a hallmark of modern luxury. We use high-grade neodymium magnets, often hidden beneath the paper wrap, to ensure a strong and satisfying seal. Our engineers carefully calculate the placement and strength of these magnets based on the size and weight of the box to prevent accidental opening during transit.
