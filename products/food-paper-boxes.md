## Product Specifications: Engineering for Safety and Functionality

When designing **custom food paper packaging**, safety is the first priority. All materials must meet strict international standards for direct food contact.

### 1. Material Selection (Food-Grade)
- **FSC Certified White Cardboard**: A bright, clean surface ideal for high-resolution printing, commonly used for pastry and confectionery boxes.
- **Kraft Paper (Virgin & Recycled)**: Known for its strength and natural look. Virgin kraft is often preferred for direct food contact.
- **Corrugated Paper (E-Flute/F-Flute)**: Provides insulation and structural strength for hot foods like pizza and burger boxes.

### 2. Specialized Coatings
- **Aqueous Coating (Water-Based)**: An eco-friendly alternative to plastic lamination, providing **oil-resistance** while remaining 100% recyclable.
- **Clay Coating**: Provides a smooth surface for superior print quality on **food-grade paper boxes**.
- **Biodegradable PE/PLA**: For items requiring a higher level of moisture resistance, though aqueous is preferred for maximum recyclability.

### 3. Box Structures
- **Tuck-Top Takeout Boxes**: The standard for delivery and takeout, designed for quick assembly.
- **Windowed Bakery Boxes**: Featuring a biodegradable film window to showcase the product inside.
- **Clamshell Containers**: One-piece designs that are easy for staff to handle and for customers to eat from.
- **Greaseproof Wraps and Liners**: Essential for fried foods and sandwiches to prevent oil leakage.

## The Importance of FSC and FDA Compliance

For food brands, certifications are not just marketing tools; they are legal requirements and trust signals.

### 1. FSC Certification
Using **FSC certified food boxes** ensures that the paper used in your packaging is sourced from responsibly managed forests. This is particularly critical for brands targeting the UK and EU markets, where environmental stewardship is highly valued.

### 2. FDA & EU Compliance
**FDA approved paper packaging** is the gold standard for food safety in the United States. Similarly, the EU has strict regulations (Regulation EC No 1935/2004) regarding materials intended to come into contact with food. Our manufacturing process ensures that all **food-grade paper boxes** are free from harmful chemicals, heavy metals, and migrants.
